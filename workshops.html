2019 CSIP Workshops

For the 2019 CSIP Student Workshops, interested sophomores and their parents were invited to come to Emeriti Hall, home of CCM's Cybersecurity Center, to participate in activities to learn about what we do and why it is essential to the cybersecurity field.

Wire Making and Raspberry Pis

  • Guests were taught how to cut, strip, order, and crimp RJ-45 wires in a lab activity. They then were guided in testing their connections with Raspberry Pis, where they were able to communicate with one another through a direct connection.

Discovering Computer Hardware

  • In this activity, guests were instructed to take apart computers in a lab activity. They learned what the internal pieces of a computer looked like, what they were called, and what they did. Then, they were challenged to put the computers back together, and test to see if they worked.

"Step By Step" Programming

  • As partners, guests were to complete an obstacle course. The catch, however, one person was blindfolded, and the other was to guide them only using simple movement commands. Although it seemed easy, any errors would cause the group to "rerun" their code and start again.

Rock, Paper, Java

  • Everyone knows how to play rock, paper, scissors. But do they know how to program it? One of the first classes CSIP students take is Computer Science I, which is based around the Java programming language. Guests were guided in building the code for a classic rock, paper, scissors game, using common methods used within the Computer Science I course.
